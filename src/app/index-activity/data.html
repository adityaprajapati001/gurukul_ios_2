Employee Promotion Process

Regulation Reference PR-01-0005

Dear Colleagues,

 

We are pleased to announce the "Employee Promotion Process," which will enhance vertical and horizontal mobility depending on the employee's performance and capabilities. While current good performance is an indicator of how well one is performing in their present role, it will not be the sole factor in promotion decisions.

 

As per the Policy, the promotion decisions will depend upon the overall capability of the employee to handle the new position. Through this policy, we strive to bring objectivity, transparency, and line of sight for career planning through a structured succession planning process, consistent with our commitment to organizational needs of delivering excellence to our end customers.

2.   Promotion Interview Panel

 

          The promotion Interview Panel is an essential part of the Promotion Process

 

Key Features

Promotion Interview Panel

Objective

To evaluate eligible candidates, recommend and approve cases of promotion

Composition

Promotion Interview Panels will be formed at the corporate centre for all the above supervisory Levels of recommended Promotion –

·         Interview Panel : Respective HOD or Authorized Authority /COO/ Head HR / Corporate HR Team

Periodicity of Meeting

·         The interview panel will meet four times a month (As per Table 5.1)

·         on account of business, exigencies meetings can be scheduled on specific requests/notifications.

Roles & Responsibilities

·         Review all eligible candidates and their data (Performance, Potential, Tenure, etc.)

·         Conduct interviews & assessments of recommended employees.

·         Approve the selected candidates for final recommendation to management.

 

3.   Process Flow – Recommendation & Selection

 

          Step 1: Eligible Positions -  HR will verify the available positions for Promotion.

 

          Step 2: Identification of Candidates - All recommendations will be initiated by the Reporting Manager.

 

          Step 3: Capability Assessment – Proper evaluation will be done by following the procedure and recommendations.

    • Assessment of Performance: Performance appraisal rating (Previous years’ rating to establish consistency of performance, if available)
    • Assessment of Potential: Potential Assessment will be done for all employees.
    • Exceptional Performance Assessment - Assessment of other criteria that will distinguish exceptional performance:
    • Model Work done - Innovation, Cross Functional projects, Customer Service (internal & external)
    • Demonstrates Value-based behaviour.

 

          Step 4: Promotion Interview Panel Assessment - All the eligible employees will be ranked in order of their Potential & Performance. The ranking will be used by the Promotion interview panel for prioritizing Promotion cases.

 

          Step 5: Final Approval
